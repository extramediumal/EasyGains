### Protein & Nutrition (Tips 1-4)

**1.** "You don't need 1g of protein per pound of bodyweight to build muscle. That's supplement marketing math. 0.7-0.8g/lb covers almost everyone."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [The Role of Protein in Your Diet](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/the-role-of-protein-in-your-diet-how-much-do-you-really-need)*

**2.** "Stop stressing about the 'anabolic window.' You don't need a shake within 30 minutes of training. Just hit your daily protein total."
*Source: Mind Pump Ep. 1757 — [The Truth About the Anabolic Window & Protein Timing](https://mindpumppodcast.com/1757-the-truth-about-the-anabolic-window-protein-timing/)*

**3.** "If you want to optimize protein, space it into 30-50g meals every 3-4 hours. But honestly? Just hitting your daily target is 90% of the game."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [The Myth of Optimal Protein Intake](https://mindpumppodcast.com/blog-post/the-myth-of-optimal-protein-intake/)*

**4.** "Falling short on protein every day? Add one shake. That's 25-50g you were missing. Whole foods first, supplements to fill gaps."
*Source: All hosts (recurring advice); [Protein Supplements Archives](https://mindpumppodcast.com/blog_category/protein-supplements/)*

### Supplements (Tips 5-7)

**5.** "Creatine monohydrate. 3-5g a day. Every day. It's the one supplement that actually works and has hundreds of studies behind it."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [Pros and Cons of Creatine](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/pros-and-cons-of-creatine)*

**6.** "Gained 3 lbs after starting creatine? Relax. It's water pulled INTO your muscles, not bloat. That's a good thing."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [Pros and Cons of Creatine](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/pros-and-cons-of-creatine)*

**7.** "Save your money on 99% of supplements. If your diet and training are solid, only creatine and caffeine have real evidence behind them."
*Source: All hosts; [Two Supplements That Can Help (a little)](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/if-diet-and-training-are-on-point-these-two-supplements-can-help-a-little)*

### Training Principles (Tips 8-11)

**8.** "Progressive overload doesn't mean adding 20 lbs to the bar. Going from 5 reps to 6 reps at the same weight? That's progress. Track it."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [Debunking Fitness Myths](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/debunking-fitness-myths-mind-pumps-no-nonsense-approach)*

**9.** "Try trigger sessions on off days: 5-10 min of light band work or bodyweight moves. Gets blood to the muscles, speeds recovery, no fatigue."
*Source: All hosts; Mind Pump Ep. 851 — [Trigger Sessions & Focus Sessions](https://mindpumppodcast.com/851-building-muscle-while-losing-fat-how-to-use-trigger-sessions-focus-sessions-to-build-muscle-male-pms-more/)*

**10.** "Your daily step count matters more than your cardio session. Aim for 8-10k steps. It burns more total calories than most people's gym cardio."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [NEAT for Fat Loss](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/does-non-exercise-activity-thermogenesis-neat-actually-help-with-fat-loss)*

**11.** "Stop 2-3 reps short of failure on most sets. You'll recover faster, train more consistently, and build more muscle long-term."
*Source: Sal Di Stefano; [Strength Training for Fat Loss](https://www.mindpumpmedia.com/blog/strength-training-with-the-goal-of-fat-loss)*

### Myth-Busting (Tips 12-16)

**12.** "Cardio doesn't burn fat. Your diet does. Cardio actually slows your metabolism over time by teaching your body to be more 'efficient.' Lift instead."

Poor sleep tanks testosterone, worsens insulin sensitivity, increases sugar cravings, and blocks neuroplasticity.

- Andy Galpin: Tips 38-42

Training to technical failure (not absolute failure) maximizes long-term gains while preserving recovery.
- Mind Pump: Tip 11

Even moderate drinking wrecks sleep architecture, raises baseline cortisol, converts testosterone to estrogen, damages the gut microbiome, and creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop.

- Mind Pump: Tips 17-18, 55
- Andy Galpin: Tips 17, 48, 56
- Huberman: Tips 15, 23

Variety of food colors = variety of micronutrients and polyphenols = more diverse gut microbiome.
