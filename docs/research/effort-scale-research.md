## 1. Mind Pump Recommendations

### Background

Mind Pump (Sal Di Stefano, Adam Schafer, Justin Andrews) is the #1 fitness podcast globally with nearly half a billion downloads. Their core philosophy is "less is more" -- they consistently argue that most people overtrain and would get better results doing less, with better quality. Sal Di Stefano authored *The Resistance Training Revolution*, which promises body transformation in "only 60 minutes a week."

### Weekly Training Frequency

- **2-3 resistance training sessions per week** is their core recommendation for average people.
- Full-body workouts are recommended for beginners, with 1-2 rest days between sessions.
- Their flagship program, MAPS Anabolic, prescribes **2-3 gym sessions per week** across a 12-week, 4-phase program.
- Sal explicitly states: "Nothing transforms your body and the way you feel more than resistance training for the average person with only 30-60 minutes twice a week."

### Session Structure

- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes per session.
- **Sets:** 9-18 working sets per body part per week (the goal is "as few sets as you can get away with to elicit results").
- **Reps:** Varied across phases -- strength phases use 5-8 reps, hypertrophy phases use 8-12 reps.
- **Rest:** 30 seconds to 3 minutes between sets. Rest is emphasized as critically important -- without adequate rest, the body shifts to endurance mode rather than muscle-building.

### Intensity and Effort Philosophy

This is where Mind Pump is most distinctive and most relevant to our app:

- **"Go MUCH easier than you think"** -- especially for beginners. If you are starting from scratch, it does not take much stimulus for your body to respond. Overdoing intensity does not accelerate results; it actually slows them.
- **Stop 1-2 reps short of failure.** Their explicit recommendation: "Stop all sets about 1-2 reps BEFORE you fail for best results." This maps to roughly an RPE 7-8 on a 0-10 scale.
- **Never train to complete failure on compound lifts** (squats, deadlifts, bench press). Reserve failure for isolation/accessory movements only, and even then sparingly.
- **Avoid forced reps, partial reps, and grinding to failure** -- all of which ultimately slow progress.
- **You should feel very little to no soreness** from your workouts, especially as a beginner. Soreness is not an indicator of a good workout.
- **"Practice exercises" rather than "work out."** The mindset should be skill development with good form, not chasing fatigue and sweat.

### Trigger Sessions (Off Days)

Mind Pump introduces "trigger sessions" -- short, low-intensity movement on non-gym days:

- **Duration:** 5-10 minutes.
- **Format:** Light band work, bodyweight exercises, mobility drills.
- **Purpose:** Get blood into muscles, maintain muscle protein synthesis signal, aid recovery.
- **Intensity:** Very low. Focus on feeling the muscle, getting a light pump, not breaking a sweat.
- This maps to roughly an RPE 2-3 on a 0-10 scale.

### NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)

Mind Pump strongly emphasizes daily walking and movement outside the gym:

- **Starting goal:** 6,000-8,000 steps per day.
- **Eventual goal:** 10,000-12,000 steps per day.
- **Approach:** Add 2,000 steps to your current baseline, make it a habit, then add more.
- This is framed as more important than cardio for fat loss and general health.

### Key Takeaway from Mind Pump

The optimal effort level for everyday people is **moderate and sustainable, not maximal**. Smart, consistent training always beats heroic one-off efforts. The person who trains at 70% effort 3x/week for a year will far outperform the person who goes 100% for 3 weeks and burns out.

## 2. Andrew Huberman / Huberman Lab Protocols

### Background

Andrew Huberman, PhD, is a neuroscientist at Stanford who hosts the Huberman Lab podcast. His "Foundational Fitness Protocol" is a detailed 7-day plan developed in collaboration with exercise physiologist Dr. Andy Galpin. It is notably more demanding than Mind Pump's recommendations, as it trains 6 out of 7 days per week.

### Huberman's 7-Day Foundational Fitness Protocol

| Day | Focus | Duration | Intensity (approx RPE) |
|-----|-------|----------|----------------------|
| Sunday (Day 1) | Long Zone 2 Cardio (jog, hike, cycle, swim) | 60-75 min | 4-5 (conversational pace) |
| Monday (Day 2) | Legs Resistance Training | 50-60 min + 10 min warmup | 7-8 (1-2 reps from failure) |
| Tuesday (Day 3) | Heat/Cold Exposure Recovery | ~60 min (5 rounds sauna + cold) | 2-3 (recovery-focused) |
| Wednesday (Day 4) | Torso Push/Pull Resistance Training | 50-60 min + 10 min warmup | 7-8 (1-2 reps from failure) |
| Thursday (Day 5) | Moderate Cardio | ~35 min | 5-6 (75-80% effort, can still talk but winded) |
| Friday (Day 6) | HIIT (sprints on bike/row) | ~20-30 min | 9-10 during work, 1-2 during rest |
| Saturday (Day 7) | Arms, Calves, Neck Resistance | 50-60 min + 10 min warmup | 7-8 |

### Resistance Training Details

- **Periodization:** Alternate monthly between Schedule A (heavy: 4-8 reps, 3-4 sets, 2-4 min rest) and Schedule B (lighter: 8-15 reps, 2-3 sets, 90 sec rest).
- **Duration:** 50-60 minutes of hard work after warmup, 75 minutes absolute maximum.
- **Exercise selection:** Two exercises per muscle group -- one emphasizing the shortened/contracted position, one emphasizing the lengthened/stretched position.
- **Frequency:** Each muscle group trained twice per week (once directly, once indirectly).
- **Final reps should feel hard**, but the protocol does not prescribe training to absolute failure.

### Cardiovascular Training

Huberman prescribes **3 cardio days per week** at different intensities:

1. **Zone 2 (long, steady):** 60-75 minutes at conversational pace. Target 180-200 minutes of Zone 2 per week total.
2. **Zone 3-4 (moderate):** ~35 minutes at 75-80% effort.
3. **HIIT (Zone 5):** 20-60 second all-out sprints with 10 second rest, 8-12 rounds.

### Recovery

- **Tuesday is a dedicated recovery day** with heat/cold contrast (sauna + ice bath).
- Wait 6-8 hours after resistance training before cold exposure to avoid blunting hypertrophy gains.
- Use **physiological sighing** (double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth) between sets and for 3-5 minutes post-workout.

### Andy Galpin's Recommendations (via Huberman Lab)

Dr. Andy Galpin, who appeared in a multi-part guest series on Huberman Lab, provides more nuanced guidelines:

- **Frequency:** 3-5 days per week for strength/power.
- **Volume:** 10 working sets per muscle group per week (beginners); up to 25 for advanced.
- **Intensity:** Work at 70%+ of 1RM for strength. Stop roughly 2 reps short of failure for hypertrophy.
- **Soreness target:** 3/10. If soreness is above 6/10, skip training that day.
- **Rest:** 3-5 minutes for strength/power, ~2 minutes for hypertrophy.
- **A full-body workout can be done in 30 minutes.**

### Scaling for Everyday People

Huberman explicitly states the protocol is a "starting point, adaptable to individual needs." Key flexibility notes:

- If feeling sick or stressed, do a shorter/lighter workout or skip and double up later.
- Bodyweight exercises or resistance bands can replace gym equipment.
- The specific calendar days are not critical -- the spacing between workout types is what matters.
- However, the protocol as written is **6 training days per week**, which is significantly more than what Mind Pump recommends and may be intimidating/unsustainable for true beginners.

### Key Takeaway from Huberman

The protocol is comprehensive and science-backed but assumes a relatively committed trainee. The most universally applicable pieces are: (1) prioritize Zone 2 cardio for longevity, (2) resistance train 2-3x/week minimum, (3) do not train to absolute failure, (4) recovery is as important as training.

## 3. RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) Deep Dive

### History and Scales

The RPE scale was developed by Swedish researcher Gunnar Borg in the 1960s. Two versions exist:

#### Original Borg Scale (6-20)

| RPE | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| 6 | No exertion at all |
| 7-8 | Extremely light |
| 9-10 | Very light |
| 11-12 | Light |
| 13-14 | Somewhat hard |
| 15-16 | Hard |
| 17-18 | Very hard |
| 19 | Extremely hard |
| 20 | Maximal exertion |

*Note: The 6-20 range was designed to roughly correlate with heart rate (multiply by 10). This is the clinical/research standard but is confusing for everyday use.*

#### Modified Borg CR10 Scale (0-10)

| RPE | Description | Practical Feel |
|-----|-------------|----------------|
| 0 | Nothing at all | Complete rest, sitting on couch |
| 1 | Very light | Casual stroll, barely feels like movement |
| 2 | Light | Easy walk, could do this for hours |
| 3 | Moderate | Brisk walk, breathing slightly elevated |
| 4 | Somewhat hard | Fast walk or easy jog, talking comfortably |
| 5 | Hard | Steady jog, can talk but with some effort |
| 6 | Harder | Running, conversation in short sentences |
| 7 | Very hard | Hard run or heavy lifting, few words at a time |
| 8 | Very, very hard | Sprinting or very heavy lifting, can barely speak |
| 9 | Near maximal | All-out effort, cannot speak |
| 10 | Maximal | Absolute limit, cannot sustain for more than seconds |

### RPE and Reps in Reserve (RIR) Relationship

In strength training contexts, RPE is often linked to Reps in Reserve:

| RPE | Reps in Reserve (RIR) | Meaning |
|-----|----------------------|---------|
| 10 | 0 | Could not do another rep (failure) |
| 9 | 1 | Could maybe do 1 more rep |
| 8 | 2 | Could do 2 more reps |
| 7 | 3 | Could do 3 more reps |
| 6 | 4+ | Several reps left, moderate effort |
| 5 | 5+ | Light-moderate, warm-up weight |

### Recommended RPE Ranges by Context

| Activity | Recommended RPE | Source |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Beginner resistance training | 6-7 | Mind Pump, general consensus |
| Intermediate resistance training | 7-8 | Mind Pump, Galpin |
| Hypertrophy sweet spot | 7-9 (1-2 RIR) | Mind Pump, Galpin |
| Zone 2 cardio | 3-5 | Huberman, general consensus |
| Moderate cardio | 5-6 | Huberman |
| HIIT work intervals | 9-10 | Huberman |
| HIIT rest intervals | 1-2 | Huberman |
| Trigger sessions / active recovery | 2-3 | Mind Pump |
| Walking / NEAT | 2-3 | Mind Pump |

### Why RPE Works for Everyday People

1. **No equipment needed.** No heart rate monitor, no 1RM testing, no math.
2. **Self-calibrating.** As fitness improves, the same absolute intensity feels easier, so the person naturally progresses.
3. **Talk test correlation.** Most people intuitively understand "can I hold a conversation?" which maps cleanly to RPE levels.
4. **Accounts for daily variation.** A bad night of sleep, stress, or illness will naturally shift RPE upward for the same workload, encouraging the person to back off when they should.

## 4. Synthesis and App Recommendations

### Is 1-10 the Right Scale?

**Yes. The 1-10 scale is ideal.** Here is the reasoning:

- The Modified Borg CR10 scale (0-10) is the established scientific standard for subjective effort rating and has been validated in decades of research.
- Both Mind Pump and Huberman's recommendations map cleanly onto a 0-10 or 1-10 framework.
- A 1-10 scale is intuitive for everyday people (everyone understands "rate this 1-10").
- The 6-20 Borg scale is confusing and only used in clinical/research settings.
- A 1-5 scale would lack sufficient granularity to distinguish between, say, a walk (2), a moderate gym session (6), and an intense session (8).

**Recommendation:** Keep the 1-10 scale. Use 0 to represent complete rest / no activity.

### What Should the Default Daily Target Be?

**The current default of 5 is reasonable but may benefit from reframing.**

Here is the reasoning based on the research:

- Mind Pump recommends training at RPE 7-8 on lifting days, but only 2-3 days per week. The other days are rest, walking, or light trigger sessions (RPE 2-3).
- Huberman's protocol has days ranging from RPE 2-3 (recovery) to RPE 7-8 (resistance training) to brief spikes of RPE 9-10 (HIIT).
- If we calculate a weekly average: 3 training days at RPE 7 + 4 rest/light days at RPE 2 = (21 + 8) / 7 = **~4.1 daily average**.
- A daily target of 5 implies the user should be doing *something moderately challenging every day*, which contradicts the "less is more" and "rest is important" messaging from both Mind Pump and Huberman.

**Recommendation:** A default daily target of **4** would be more aligned with the research. However, the more important insight is that daily targets are less meaningful than weekly patterns (which already aligns with our app's "weekly view is primary" philosophy).

### Recommended Weekly Pattern for Everyday People

Based on the convergence between Mind Pump and Huberman, here is a realistic weekly pattern for an average person:

#### Beginner (Just Starting Out)

| Day | Activity | Effort Score |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Monday | Resistance Training (full body, 30-45 min) | 6 |
| Tuesday | Walk (30-60 min) + light stretching | 2 |
| Wednesday | Rest or very light movement | 1 |
| Thursday | Resistance Training (full body, 30-45 min) | 6 |
| Friday | Walk (30-60 min) | 2 |
| Saturday | Fun activity (hike, bike, sports, yard work) | 4 |
| Sunday | Rest | 0 |

**Weekly total: ~21 | Weekly average: ~3.0**

#### Intermediate (3-6 months of consistent training)

| Day | Activity | Effort Score |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Monday | Resistance Training (45-60 min) | 7 |
| Tuesday | Zone 2 Cardio (30-45 min walk/jog) | 4 |
| Wednesday | Resistance Training (45-60 min) | 7 |
| Thursday | Light cardio or trigger session (15-30 min) | 3 |
| Friday | Resistance Training (45-60 min) | 7 |
| Saturday | Moderate cardio or active hobby (45-60 min) | 5 |
| Sunday | Rest or gentle walk | 1 |

**Weekly total: ~34 | Weekly average: ~4.9**

#### Consistent / Committed (6+ months)

| Day | Activity | Effort Score |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Monday | Resistance Training - Legs (60 min) | 8 |
| Tuesday | Zone 2 Cardio (45-60 min) | 4 |
| Wednesday | Resistance Training - Upper Push/Pull (60 min) | 8 |
| Thursday | Moderate Cardio (30-35 min) | 5 |
| Friday | HIIT (20-25 min) | 9 |
| Saturday | Resistance Training - Arms/Accessories (45 min) | 7 |
| Sunday | Long Zone 2 walk/hike (60+ min) | 3 |

**Weekly total: ~44 | Weekly average: ~6.3**

### How to Message Effort to Non-Gym-Rats

Based on the Mind Pump philosophy (which best aligns with EasyGains' "chill buddy" tone), here are messaging guidelines:

#### Core Principles

1. **Rest days are not zero days -- they are recovery days.** Frame rest as productive, not lazy. "Your muscles grow when you rest, not when you lift."

2. **A 6-7 is a GREAT workout.** Most people think they need to be at 9-10 to "count." The research unanimously says RPE 7-8 (1-2 reps in reserve) is the sweet spot for building muscle. Pushing to 10 every session actually slows progress.

3. **Walking counts.** A 30-minute walk is a legitimate RPE 2-3 entry. NEAT/daily movement is more important for fat loss than cardio sessions. Mind Pump explicitly recommends tracking daily steps.

4. **Consistency beats intensity.** The #1 predictor of fitness results is showing up regularly at moderate effort, not occasionally crushing yourself. A year of 6s beats a month of 10s.

5. **Soreness is not a badge of honor.** If you are regularly sore above a 3/10, you are overdoing it (per Andy Galpin's guidelines). The app should never encourage users to push through significant soreness.

#### Effort Score Descriptions (User-Facing)

| Score | Label | Description | Example |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|
| 0 | Rest | No intentional activity | Couch day, and that is fine |
| 1 | Minimal | Barely moved, light stretching | 5-min stretch before bed |
| 2 | Easy | Light movement, no sweat | Casual walk, light housework |
| 3 | Light | Moving with purpose, breathing easy | Brisk walk, yoga, trigger session |
| 4 | Moderate | Noticeable effort, could do this a while | Long walk, easy bike ride, light lifting |
| 5 | Steady | Working, slightly winded, holding conversation | Jogging, moderate gym session |
| 6 | Challenging | Pushing yourself, conversation gets harder | Solid workout, most reps felt good |
| 7 | Hard | Real effort, talking in short sentences | Strong gym session, 1-2 reps left in the tank |
| 8 | Very Hard | Near your limit for sustained work | Heavy lifting day, intense class |
| 9 | Intense | Almost all-out, hard to speak | Sprint intervals, PR attempts |
| 10 | Max | Everything you had, could not do more | Competition, true all-out effort (rare) |

#### Messaging Tone Examples (EasyGains Voice)

- **After logging a 3:** "Light movement day? That is solid. Recovery is where the gains happen."
- **After logging a 6-7:** "That is the sweet spot. Consistent effort like this is what builds real results."
- **After logging a 9-10 multiple days in a row:** "You are going hard! Just remember -- your body builds muscle during rest, not during the workout. A couple of easier days might actually speed up your progress."
- **After a rest day (0):** "Rest day logged. Your muscles are thanking you right now."
- **After 3+ days of 0:** "Hey, even a short walk counts. Shall we aim for a 2-3 tomorrow?"

### Recommended Default Settings

| Setting | Value | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Default daily effort target | 4 | Reflects realistic average across training + rest days |
| Recommended training days/week | 3 | Mind Pump's core recommendation for average people |
| Recommended effort on training days | 6-7 | RPE 7-8 for intermediates, 6-7 for beginners (1-2 RIR) |
| Recommended effort on rest days | 1-3 | Walking, stretching, trigger sessions |
| Weekly effort target (beginner) | 20-25 | ~3 days at 6 + light movement other days |
| Weekly effort target (intermediate) | 30-35 | ~3 days at 7 + moderate activity other days |
| Weekly effort target (committed) | 40-45 | ~4-5 days at 7-8 + active recovery |

### Key Insight: Weekly > Daily

Both Mind Pump and Huberman think in terms of **weekly volume and patterns**, not daily targets. This perfectly aligns with EasyGains' design decision to make the weekly view the primary metric view.

The app should:
1. Show daily effort as a simple number entry (tap or voice).
2. Show the **weekly pattern** as the primary feedback mechanism (bar chart of daily scores).
3. Celebrate **consistency of the weekly pattern** over any single day's score.
4. Never shame a 0 day if the weekly pattern includes adequate training days.
5. Gently nudge if the weekly total falls below the user's target range.
