## Key Findings

### Pricing Psychology
- "Under $50/year" and "under $5/month" is a double psychological win
- $6.99/mo is justified by the AI/voice differentiator — still 46% below H&F median ($12.99)
- 18% annual discount is too thin; industry standard is 25-40%
- 67-69% of fitness subscribers choose annual — optimize for this

### Trial vs Freemium
- Freemium median conversion: 2.18%
- Trial-to-paid in H&F: 39.9% (highest of any category)
- Recommendation: 7-day full Pro trial → drop to free tier
- 56% of H&F apps use this hybrid model

### Unit Economics at Recommended Pricing ($6.99/mo, $49.99/yr)

Blended revenue (65% annual / 35% monthly): ~$4.82/mo per user

## Recommended Launch Configuration

| Element | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Monthly | $6.99/mo |
| Annual | $49.99/yr ($4.17/mo, 40% off) |
| Introductory offer | 7-day free trial (full Pro access) |
| Free tier (post-trial) | 3-5 entries/day, no clarifications, no reminders |
| Founding lifetime | $99.99 (first 200 users only) |
| Paywall timing | Show on first open, pre-select annual |

### Additional Recommendations
- Consider bumping free tier to 5 entries/day (lets users see a complete day)
- Weekly plan at $1.99/week as optional test (low commitment, higher effective annual)
- Never paywall something that was previously free (MFP's #1 mistake)
- MFP user frustration is a direct acquisition channel — position against it
- Coach AL personality + weekly focus = natural anti-churn strategy
